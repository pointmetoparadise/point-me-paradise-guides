A Kauaʻi Dining Guide — The Tables Worth Planning For

Kauaʻi's dining scene rewards those who plan ahead. The island's best restaurants are small, seasonal, and in high demand — the tables worth sitting at are rarely available on a whim. Reserve early, reserve often, and let the food be part of the adventure.

Hanalei Town
Bar Acuda
Hanalei · 5-5161 Kuhio Hwy
Reservation Required
Mediterranean TapasLocally SourcedOutstanding Wine ListTue – Sat Only

The North Shore's most coveted dinner reservation — and deservedly so. Chef-owners Hanna and Kenny Uddifa run a tight, brilliantly executed tapas program out of a small, warm room in Hanalei. The menu changes with the season; regulars return for the local cucumber salad, lamb riblets, honeycomb plate with Humboldt Fog, house-made pasta specials, and the unforgettable lilikoi cheesecake. The wine list — weighted toward Rhône varietals and small-production European bottles — is exceptional for a restaurant this size on an island this remote.

Reserve exactly 30 days in advance — that's when the booking window opens on Resy. Turn on cancellation alerts if your dates show full. Bar seating (21+) is first come, first served. Tuesday through Saturday only.

Postcards Café
Hanalei · 5-5075 Kuhio Hwy · Plantation cottage
Reservation Recommended
Global Fine DiningOcean-to-TableOrganic IngredientsTue – Sat Only

Tucked into a charming 1860s plantation cottage on the edge of Hanalei, Postcards is one of the North Shore's best fine dining secrets. Chef Javier Melendez draws from his global travels — French and Japanese technique layered with flavors from Asia, Africa, and the Caribbean — all grounded in organic, locally sourced ingredients. Wagyu NY strip, coriander-crusted ahi, tuna on watermelon with chili and jalapeño, grilled octopus, chèvre cheesecake. The screened porch catches the last of the Hanalei sunset beautifully.

The Dolphin Restaurant — Hanalei First Come, First Served
A Hanalei institution since the 1970s. The Dolphin does not take reservations — arrive before 6pm for the best chance at a table in the riverside dining room or a seat in the world-class sushi lounge. Known for the freshest local seafood, Kailani Farms organic greens, and a serene Hanalei River setting that hasn't changed in 50 years. Worth the wait. Arrive early.

Āina Kauaʻi
Kapaʻa · 4-985 Kuhio Hwy
Reservation Required
Kaiseki Tasting Menu14 Seats OnlyThu & Sat OnlyHale ʻAina Best Restaurant 2023–2025

The most celebrated dining experience on Kauaʻi — and one of the most intimate tasting menus in all of Hawaiʻi. Chef Mitch Muroff seats just 14 guests per evening, executing a nine-course kaiseki tasting menu that layers Japanese technique over the island's seasonal harvest. Every course arrives simultaneously, plated in real time in full view. The beverage program centers on premium sake, Japanese whisky, and local spirits. This is not a dinner — it's a ceremony.

No modifications. No substitutions. No cancellations. Dietary restrictions must be emailed before booking. Thursday and Saturday only, one seating per night.

Hualani's at Timbers Kauaʻi
Līhuʻe · 3770 Alaʻoli Way · Open to the public
Reservation RecommendedResort Dining
Oceanfront Farm-to-TableOn-Site Organic FarmBreakfast · Lunch · DinnerOpenTable Diners' Choice 2024

Perched above Nawiliwili Bay at Timbers Kauaʻi, Hualani's is an OpenTable Diners' Choice winner for Best Overall, Best Food, Best Ambiance, and Best Service. The kitchen draws directly from The Farm at Hōkūala — the resort's on-site organic farm — plus local fishermen, ranchers, and artisan producers. Deeply seasonal, deeply Kauaʻi. Signature dishes: lobster tempura, filet mignon, whole fried fish, and the legendary lilikoi cheesecake. Whale sightings from the terrace in season. Complimentary valet. Perfect first or last night given proximity to Līhuʻe airport.

Reservation RecommendedResort Dining
Pacific Rim Fine DiningChef's Table AvailableMonthly Paʻina Tasting DinnersWeekend Brunch

Tucked inside the intimate Koʻa Kea boutique resort, Red Salt is one of Kauaʻi's most quietly excellent restaurants. Inspired by Kauaʻi's sacred ʻalaea red salt, Chef Noelani Planas (trained under Joel Robuchon and Wolfgang Puck) brings a sophisticated Pacific Rim lens to local ingredients. The monthly "Dinner Paʻina Series" — a multi-course tasting dinner in a private oceanfront cabana with sommelier wine pairings — is an exceptional special-occasion experience. Sushi bar select evenings (walk-in only).
